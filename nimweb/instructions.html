Playing The Game

That's great," you say. "But what about actually playing the game?"

I did say we were going to play, didn't I?

This version of Nim is quite simple. There are two players - a human (you, the player) and a simple computer. The Nim pile starts with 7 sticks. The human player can take up to 3 sticks from the pile per turn. The simple computer can take only one stick from the pile per turn.

When the game starts, neither the human or computer are allowed to play yet. You (the human) must decide which player goes first.

To start play, decide whether the human or computer is going to go first.

Human Goes First

Computer Goes First

When it's the human's turn, choose the number of sticks the human takes from the pile (1 to 3) and select "Take Sticks". When it's the computer's turn, select "Take Turn". One stick will be removed from the pile. Play continues until there is one stick left. The player that leaves the last stick in the pile WINS the game!
