History

Nim has a colorful and storied history. While there is no definite timeline as to when Nim was created, there are indications that a game similar to Nim called 捡石子 (jiǎn-shízi), or "picking stones" was played in ancient China.

European references to a game like Nim began to appear in the 16th century. The game was played with stones, marbles, sticks, or anything players had available.

Today's game of Nim was named by Dr. Charles L. Bouton of Harvard University. In 1901, Dr. Bouton developed the theory of Nim that is still in use today.
