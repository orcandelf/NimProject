Other Nim Examples

There are actually several different types of Nim. Standard Nim is played "mis√®re", meaning that the player that takes the last object loses the match.

Nim is played starting with piles or rows of objects. Players take turns taking any number of objects from one of the piles.

For this example game, we will be using 16 objects in rows of 7, 5, 3, and 1.

Our two players are "Nancy" and "Bill". "Nancy" goes first. She chooses to remove 2 from the row of 7.

"Bill" then takes all 5 from the row of 5.

"Nancy" then takes the object in row 1.

"Bill" then takes 3 from the row of 7,

"Nancy" takes all 3 objects from the row of 3.

Even though we know the outcome now, we'll continue playing. "Bill" takes 1 object from the row of 7.

Poor "Nancy" loses this game. She takes the last object.

See, easy peasy! From these simple instructions, you can play several versions of Nim like:

  • Straight Nim: The winner is the player who takes the last counter
  • The 21 Game: Rather than using counters or objects, use numbers up to 21. The first player says "1" and each player in turn increases the number by 1, 2, or 3, but may not exceed 21; the player forced to say "21" loses.
  • Circular Nim: Objects are placed in a circle instead of in piles. Players remove 1, 2, or 3 adjacent objects. The player who chooses the last object(s) loses.
  • Greedy Nim: Players must chose objects from the pile with the most objects.
  • Building Nim: Players build their piles one object at a time before beginning game play.
